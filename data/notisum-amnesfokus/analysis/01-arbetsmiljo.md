# Arbetsmiljo -- Laglistaanalys

## 1. Overview

The Arbetsmiljo law list is the most comprehensive occupational health and safety compliance register available through laglig.se. It contains **112 documents** organized into **9 sections**, covering the full breadth of Swedish workplace regulation -- from foundational framework legislation down to specific technical provisions for individual hazard types.

**Purpose:** To provide employers, safety officers (skyddsombud), and compliance professionals with a complete, structured inventory of every statute, regulation, and binding provision that governs workplace health, safety, and employment conditions in Sweden and the EU.

**Target audience:** All Swedish employers regardless of industry, with particular relevance for manufacturing, construction, transport, and chemical-handling sectors. The list is equally valuable for HR departments, occupational health services (foretagshalsovard), and building owners/managers.

**Scope:** The list spans the entire Swedish legal hierarchy -- riksdag-level laws (lagar), government ordinances (forordningar), agency-level provisions (foreskrifter from Arbetsmiljoverket, MSB, Elsak, Boverket, Kemikalieinspektionen, Skatteverket, and others), and directly applicable EU regulations. It covers physical, organizational, social, and chemical working environments, as well as adjacent areas like fire safety, electrical safety, transport of dangerous goods, and radiation protection.

**Key statistics:**

- 112 documents total
- 9 thematic sections (numbered 01-09)
- Approximately 20 distinct regulatory bodies referenced
- 5 directly applicable EU regulations
- All Arbetsmiljoverket provisions updated to the AFS 2023-series (effective 1 January 2025)
- 55 of these 112 laws also appear in the subset list "Arbetsmiljo for tjansteforetag"
- Significant overlaps with Miljo Sverige (37 shared), Miljo (22 shared), Fastighet-Bygg (9 shared), and Halsa och sjukvard (4 shared)

### 3.4 AFS 2023 consolidation

A major observation is that Arbetsmiljoverket consolidated its entire provision portfolio into a new series effective 1 January 2025. The list uses "ersatter" references to map old AFS numbers to new ones. The main consolidation containers are:

- **AFS 2023:1** -- Systematic work environment management (standalone)
- **AFS 2023:2** -- Multiple chapters covering OSA, lone work, youth labor, pregnancy, first aid, violence, working time records

- **AFS 2023:5** -- Pressure-bearing equipment (products)

## 4. Content Pattern Assessment

### 4.1 Expert commentary coverage

Based on the CSV data, the content coverage is remarkably comprehensive:

- **100% of documents** have an expert commentary field populated (the "Notisum Comment" column). Every one of the 112 entries contains a substantive description explaining the provision's scope and key requirements.
- **~95% of documents** have a compliance guidance field populated (the "Sa har paverkas vi" column). Nearly every entry includes obligation-oriented compliance text written from the employer's perspective.
- **0% of documents** have the supplementary "Compliance" column populated -- this column is uniformly empty across the entire list, suggesting it was reserved for a feature that was never activated.

### 4.2 Content quality observations

- The expert commentaries vary in length from brief one-paragraph overviews (for simpler ordinances) to multi-page detailed breakdowns (for AFS 2023:3 on construction safety, which covers 11 chapters).
- Compliance guidance uses a consistent "Vi ska..." voice pattern, making it directly usable as an employer-facing checklist.
- Some documents (particularly in Section 09) reference multiple chapters of the same AFS provision, leading to conceptual overlap with entries in Sections 03-08. For example, AFS 2023:10 chapter 7 on chemical risk sources appears as a referenced context in many Section 09 entries.

### 4.3 Identified gaps

- **Link column:** All documents have a Notisum link, but these point to a proprietary platform rather than to official government sources (riksdagen.se, av.se).
- **Amendment tracking:** The "Amendment SFS" column is populated for about 50% of entries (primarily riksdag-level laws), but is consistently empty for AFS provisions, which instead use the "ersatter" notation in the reference number.
- **No effective-date field:** Although many commentaries mention "Ikrafttraddande 1 januari 2025," there is no structured field for effective dates.

## 5. Laglig.se Recommendations

### 5.1 Improved categorization scheme

The current 9-section structure is workable but could be improved for laglig.se's purposes:

1. **Section 09 is oversized.** At 43 documents (38% of the list), it functions as a catch-all. We recommend splitting it into at least 4 sub-categories:
   - **Transport and posted workers** (SFS 1999:678, SFS 1994:1297, SFS 2004:865, SFS 2005:395, SFS 2006:263, SFS 2006:311, (EG) nr 561/2006, (EU) nr 165/2014, MSBFS 2015:9, SFS 2017:319, SFS 2022:469, MSBFS 2024:10) -- 12 documents
   - **Radiation, infection, and biological hazards** (SFS 2018:396, SFS 2018:506, AFS 2023:10 smittrisker, AFS 2023:10 artificiell optisk stralning) -- 4 documents
   - **Advanced chemical and physical hazards** (AFS 2023:10 gaser, kvarts, explosionsfarlig miljo, bekampningsmedel, syntetiska fibrer, smaltsvetsning, AFS 2023:14 gransvarden, AFS 2023:13 asbest, (EU) nr 1021/2019, KIFS 2022:3) -- 10 documents
   - **Specialized equipment and construction supplements** (AFS 2023:11 stallningar, motorsagar, personlyft, lyftanordningar, besiktning, pressar, AFS 2023:5 tryckbarande, AFS 2023:2 vald och hot) -- 9 documents
   - **Miscellaneous** (SFS 2003:789 skydd mot olyckor forordning, SFS 2007:19 PCB, MSBFS 2013:3 tillstand brandfarliga, SKVFS 2015:6 personalliggare, ELSAK-FS 2017:2 and 2017:3, MSBFS 2025:2 explosiva varor) -- 7 documents

2. **Section 02 conflates labor law with OHS.** While HR law is legitimately connected to occupational health, a cleaner split would separate employment relationship laws (LAS, MBL, leave laws) from workplace-specific HR compliance (GDPR, whistleblower, working time).

3. **Electrical safety additions from Section 09 should join Section 07.** ELSAK-FS 2017:2 and ELSAK-FS 2017:3 belong logically with the other electrical safety provisions in Section 07.

## 7. Agent Training Annotations

### 7.1 Classification rules for assigning laws to sections

When an AI agent encounters a new law or provision and must assign it to a section in this list, the following decision tree applies:

1. **Is it a framework-level statute or provision that applies to all employers regardless of industry?** -> Section 01 (Grundlaggande regelverk). Examples: AML, SAM, OSA, medical examinations.

2. **Does it primarily regulate the employment relationship, worker rights, or personal data?** -> Section 02 (Arbetsratt och personalforvaltning). Examples: LAS, MBL, leave laws, GDPR.

3. **Does it regulate how work activities are organized, or address specific worker groups (pregnant, young, lone)?** -> Section 03 (Arbetsplatsens utformning). Also includes construction safety coordination roles.

4. **Does it target physical loads on the body (ergonomics, noise, vibration) or building climate systems?** -> Section 04 (Fysisk belastning och lokalforhallanden).

5. **Does it regulate a specific type of work equipment, machine, or tool (including display screens)?** -> Section 05 (Maskiner, utrustning och tekniska hjalpmedel).

6. **Does it relate to fire prevention, flammable/explosive substances, or pressurized equipment?** -> Section 06 (Brand, explosion och trycksatta anordningar).

7. **Does it relate to electrical installations, electrical equipment safety, or electromagnetic fields?** -> Section 07 (Elinstallationer och saker drift).

8. **Does it regulate chemical substances, chemical products, or chemical risk assessment in the workplace?** -> Section 08 (Kemiska risker och farliga amnen).

9. **Does it not fit cleanly into any of the above?** -> Section 09 (Kompletterande bestammelser). This includes transport-specific rules, radiation protection, infection control, specialized equipment, tax-related personnel requirements, and other cross-cutting provisions.

### 7.2 Index numbering convention

The list uses a **SSDD format** where:

- **SS** = two-digit section number (01-09)
- **DD** = two-digit document sequence within the section (00, 10, 20, ...)

The standard increment is 10 (0100, 0110, 0120, ...) to leave room for insertions. When a document is added between two existing entries, the final digit is used (e.g., 0381 was inserted after 0380). Multiple documents may share the same index when they represent complementary product-side and user-side provisions for the same subject (e.g., 0520 for both AFS 2023:12 and AFS 2023:11 on display screens).

Section 09 documents have **no index numbers** assigned in the source data, meaning they were appended without being individually sequenced.

### 7.3 Original compliance summary examples

The following examples demonstrate laglig.se's voice for employer-facing compliance summaries. These are original formulations -- not reproductions of any existing text.

**Example 1 -- Framework law (Arbetsmiljolag):**

> Vi behover ha ett fungerande systematiskt arbetsmiljoarbete som omfattar alla delar av var verksamhet. Vi ska kontinuerligt undersoka arbetsmiljon, bedoma risker, vidta atgarder och folja upp resultaten. Om nagot allvarligt intraffar -- dodsfall, svara skador eller allvarliga tillbud -- ska vi omedelbart anmala detta till Arbetsmiljoverket. Pa arbetsplatser med fler an fem anstallda ska vi utse skyddsombud, och vid minst 50 anstallda ska vi inratta en skyddskommitte.

**Example 2 -- Technical provision (Buller, AFS 2023:10):**

> Vi ska sakerstalla att ingen arbetstagare utsatts for bullerniva over gallande gransvarden. Det innebar att vi behover genomfora bullermatningar, riskbedoma resultaten och vidta atgarder -- i forsta hand genom att minska bullret vid kallan. Om exponering overskrider de undre insatsvaerdena ska vi tillhandahalla horselskydd och erbjuda horselundersokning. Alla berorda arbetstagare ska fa information om bullerrisker.

**Example 3 -- EU regulation (REACH):**

> Vi behover kartlagga vilka kemiska amnen vi anvander i var verksamhet och sakerstalla att samtliga ar registrerade i enlighet med REACH-forordningen. Som nedstromanvandare ska vi folja de anvandarvillkor som anges i sakerhetsdata-bladen. Vi ska kontrollera att inga amnen pa forordningens tillstandslista (Bilaga XIV) anvands utan giltigt tillstand och att eventuella begransningsregler i Bilaga XVII efterlevs.

**Example 4 -- Employment law (Diskrimineringslag):**

> Vi ska sakerstalla att ingen arbetstagare, arbetssokande eller inhyrd personal diskrimineras pa grund av kon, alder, funktionsnedsattning, etnisk tillhorighet, religion, sexuell laggning eller konsoverskridande identitet. Vi behover bedriva ett aktivt forebyggande arbete mot diskriminering och trakasserier, inklusive lonetransparensarbete och lonekartlaggning. Dokumenterade atgardsplaner kravs for arbetsgivare med 25 eller fler anstallda.

### 7.4 Key patterns for agent training

- **Obligation-focused voice:** All summaries should begin with "Vi behover..." or "Vi ska..." to frame requirements as employer obligations.
- **Action-oriented structure:** Each summary should identify the concrete actions required (investigate, document, report, appoint, train, provide).
- **Threshold awareness:** Many provisions have employee-count thresholds (5 for safety representatives, 10 for written documentation requirements, 25 for action plans, 50 for safety committees). These should be highlighted.
- **Cross-reference awareness:** The AFS 2023 consolidation means many provisions are chapters within a single AFS. Agents should note which AFS and chapter a provision belongs to, as users may encounter a different entry point into the same document.
- **Amendment tracking:** Always note the "ersatter" reference to help users who still have documentation referencing old AFS numbers (e.g., "AFS 2023:1 ersatter AFS 2001:1" means the old SAM provisions now live in the new document).
