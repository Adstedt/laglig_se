# Informationssakerhet Sverige -- Laglistaanalys

## 1. Overview

This list contains **42 documents** governing information security, data protection, and digital-infrastructure obligations in Sweden. The source defines five thematic section headings but fails to distribute documents among them -- all 42 documents are placed in the last section ("05"), while sections 01 through 04 remain empty. The five source headings are:

1. Personuppgifter och integritet
2. Informationssakerhet och incidentrapportering
3. Sakerhetsskydd och nationell sakerhet
4. Elektronisk kommunikation och digital infrastruktur
5. Sektorsspecifika krav och finansiell motstandskraft

## 4. Content Pattern Assessment

### Structural patterns

- **Multi-layer implementation stacks:** Most EU instruments are followed by a Swedish law, ordinance, and one or more agency prescriptions (e.g., NIS directive leads to SFS 2018:1174 + SFS 2018:1175 + MSBFS 2018:8/9/10/11)
- **Sector-specific supplements:** The general NIS/security-protection framework is extended by sector prescriptions from energy (STEMFS, SvKFS), telecom (PTSFS), transport (TSFS), and finance (FFFS)
- **Cross-regime overlap:** GDPR, the public-access/secrecy act, and the NIS law sit at the intersection of data protection, transparency, and cybersecurity -- creating complex compliance interactions

### Unique characteristics

- The list is one of the few that covers three distinct regulatory regimes (data protection, cybersecurity, national security) in a single inventory
- The AI Act ((EU) 2024/1689) is a recent addition reflecting the expanding scope of information-security compliance
- SvKFS 2022:1 (security protection for electricity) and SvKFS 2023:1 (electricity preparedness) target the same operators but under different legal bases -- this distinction should be clear in user guidance

### Gap analysis

- **No coverage of the forthcoming Swedish NIS 2 transposition act** -- when published (expected 2025-2026), it will replace SFS 2018:1174/1175 and MSBFS 2018:8-11
- **No coverage of the CER transposition** -- 2022/2557/EU requires a Swedish implementing act
- **No coverage of Cyber Resilience Act (CRA)** -- (EU) 2024/2847 will add product-security obligations relevant to this list
- **Limited financial-sector coverage** -- only FFFS 2014:5 and DORA are present; FI has issued updated regulations (FFFS 2024:11) that should be tracked

## 5. Laglig.se Recommendations

### Section naming strategy

Use the laglig.se section names proposed in Section 2 above:

1. **Dataskydd och personlig integritet** (our name for the personal-data regime)
2. **Systematisk informationssakerhet och incidenthantering** (our name for the NIS/cybersecurity regime)
3. **Sakerhetsskydd och rikets sakerhet** (our name for the security-protection regime)
4. **Elektroniska kommunikationstjanster och digital tillit** (our name for the e-com/digital identity space)
5. **Branschspecifika informationskrav** (our name for domain-specific obligations)

### Compliance summary approach

For information-security professionals, frame compliance obligations around four dimensions:

1. **Dataskydd** -- GDPR, complementary law, camera surveillance, guidance
2. **Cybersakerhet** -- NIS, incident reporting, risk analysis, AI governance
3. **Sakerhetsskydd** -- classified information, personnel vetting, security-protection agreements
4. **Digital tillit** -- eIDAS, electronic communications, e-commerce, domain governance

### Priority ordering for agents

When an agent identifies a user as an information-security officer or DPO, present sections in this order:

1. Section A (data protection -- affects all organisations)
2. Section B (cybersecurity -- affects NIS-regulated entities and digital-service providers)
3. Section D (digital trust -- affects organisations offering e-services or using e-ID)
4. Section C (national security -- affects only security-sensitive operations)
5. Section E (sector-specific -- conditional on industry)

### Transition alerts

- Flag SFS 2018:1174/1175 and MSBFS 2018:8-11 as "pending NIS 2 replacement"
- Flag (EU) 2024/1689 as "phased application from February 2025"
- Flag (EU) 2022/2554 (DORA) as "applies from 17 January 2025"
