# Arbetsrätt och personal — Laglistaanalys

## 1. Overview

This list contains **64 documents** and is currently UNCATEGORIZED — all documents are placed in a single flat section with no index numbers.

**Critical naming mismatch:** Despite being named "Miljö Sverige" in the source, this list has **nothing to do with environmental legislation**. It is a comprehensive **workplace law and HR compliance** package covering employment protection, working hours, leave entitlements, work environment regulations, discrimination, whistleblowing, data protection, and social insurance. The "Miljö" in the name likely refers to "arbetsmiljö" (work environment) rather than "yttre miljö" (external environment).

**Content profile:**

- 19 SFS laws covering employment relationships (LAS, MBL, Semesterlag, Arbetstidslag, etc.)
- 12 leave-of-absence entitlements (parental, study, sick, family, SFI, etc.)
- 12 AFS work environment regulations (SAM, OSA, workplace design, equipment, PPE, etc.)
- 6 whistleblowing and transparency rules
- 5 data protection and privacy documents (GDPR + Swedish supplements)
- 4 social insurance and sick pay rules
- 3 transport-sector working time rules
- 2 checklists (office safety rounds, workplace adaptation)
- 1 EU pay transparency directive

**Overlap with other lists:**

- 37 documents shared with the Arbetsmiljö list
- 30 documents shared with the Arbetsmiljö tjänsteföretag list

## 4. Content Pattern Assessment

### The AFS 2023 consolidation

The list reflects Arbetsmiljöverket's major 2023 regulatory consolidation where dozens of older AFS were merged into three main regulation packages:

- **AFS 2023:1** = SAM (previously AFS 2001:1)
- **AFS 2023:2** = Multiple chapters covering OSA, adaptation, young workers, pregnant workers, first aid, overtime records (previously 6+ separate AFS)
- **AFS 2023:10-15** = Physical environment regulations (workplace design, equipment, PPE, noise, medical exams)

The list correctly references the new AFS numbers with "(ersätter AFS YYYY:N)" notation, which is important for agents to handle search queries using either old or new numbers.

### Leave-of-absence cluster

Sweden has an unusually large number of statutory leave entitlements. This list captures 8 separate leave laws:

- Parental leave (SFS 1995:584)
- Study leave (SFS 1974:981)
- Care of sick relative (SFS 1988:1465)
- Family emergency (SFS 1998:209)
- SFI language courses (SFS 1986:163)
- Sick leave — try other work (SFS 2008:565)
- Government employee leave (SFS 1984:111)
- Union representative leave (via SFS 1974:358)

### Data completeness issues

- Several documents lack `notisumComment` and `summaryText` (e.g., SFS 2018:42, SFS 2016:157, SFS 2016:145, SFS 1994:260, SFS 1987:1245, SFS 1984:111)
- The two checklists have no commentary at all
- This means laglig.se will need to generate original summaries for approximately 8-10 documents

### Naming confusion — critical for agents

An agent that receives a query about "Miljö Sverige" must understand:

- This list is about **workplace and HR law**, NOT environmental regulations
- The actual environmental list is "Miljö" or "Miljö för tjänsteföretag"
- Agent routing should map "arbetsmiljö", "personal", "HR", "anställning" queries to this list
- Agent routing should NOT map "miljö", "avfall", "kemikalier" queries here

### Priority ordering for agents

When presenting to a new employer:

1. **Section C** (SAM/OSA) — mandatory for ALL employers from day one
2. **Section A** (LAS) — employment contracts are the first legal relationship
3. **Section B** (working hours/leave) — daily operational compliance
4. **Section E** (discrimination/whistleblowing) — active measures required annually
5. **Section D** (medical/insurance) — triggered by incidents and specific exposures
6. **Section F** (GDPR) — applies from first recruitment
7. **Section G** (union/authorities) — relevant when employees are unionized or inspections occur

```
compliance_context: "employer_data_protection"
trigger_phrases: ["GDPR", "personuppgifter", "dataskydd", "samtycke", "integritet", "personuppgiftsbehandling", "registerförteckning"]
```

**Example compliance summaries (laglig.se originals):**

- **(EU) nr 679/2016 GDPR:** "Vi ska behandla medarbetares och kunders personuppgifter i enlighet med dataskyddsförordningen. Det innebär att vi behöver dokumenterade rättsliga grunder för varje behandling, tydlig information till de registrerade, och tekniska och organisatoriska skyddsåtgärder."

- **SFS 2018:218:** "Vi ska tillämpa den svenska kompletteringslagen till GDPR. Den anger bland annat att barn från 13 års ålder kan samtycka till behandling av personuppgifter vid digitala tjänster, och reglerar behandling av känsliga uppgifter i arbetsrättsliga sammanhang."

### Section G: Fackliga rättigheter, arbetsdomstol och myndighetskontroll

```
compliance_context: "employer_collective_relations_authorities"
trigger_phrases: ["MBL", "förhandling", "kollektivavtal", "facklig", "skyddsombud", "personalliggare", "arbetstillstånd", "arbetsdomstolen"]
```

**Example compliance summaries (laglig.se originals):**

- **SFS 1976:580 MBL:** "Vi ska förhandla med berörda fackförbund innan vi fattar beslut om viktigare förändringar i verksamheten eller för enskilda medarbetare. Vi har informationsskyldighet och ska lämna de uppgifter som behövs för att facket ska kunna fullgöra sin roll."

- **SFS 1974:358:** "Vi ska ge fackliga förtroendemän den ledighet som behövs för deras uppdrag och tillgång till lokal för fackligt arbete. En facklig förtroendeman får inte ges sämre villkor än andra anställda på grund av sitt uppdrag."

- **SKVFS 2015:6:** "Vi ska föra personalliggare om vi bedriver verksamhet inom restaurang, frisör, bygg, fordonsservice, kropps-/skönhetsvård, tvätteri eller livsmedels-/tobaksgrossist. Liggaren ska vara lättåtkomlig vid Skatteverkets kontrollbesök och inkludera alla verksamma — även oavlönade och inhyrda."
