# Rapport (report)

A rapport documents an outcome at a point in time — an utredning, revision,
uppföljning or sammanställning. It is descriptive and dated, not normative.

## Structure

1. **Sammanfattning** — the conclusions in 3–5 sentences, readable standalone.
2. **Bakgrund och syfte** — what prompted the report, the question it answers, the
   period/scope it covers.
3. **Underlag och metod** — what was examined (dokument, intervjuer, mätningar,
   platsbesök).
4. **Iakttagelser/resultat** — the findings, grouped per area. Tables welcome for
   quantitative material.
5. **Slutsatser och rekommendationer** — what follows from the findings;
   recommendations clearly separated from facts.

## Style

- Past tense for what was done/found ("granskningen visade…"), present for
  conclusions ("rutinen uppfyller därmed…").
- Facts and bedömningar visibly separated — a reader must see which is which.
- Dated: the report states its period and date of writing.

## Criteria

- Sammanfattning is consistent with the body (no conclusions appearing only in the
  summary).
- Every rekommendation traces to an iakttagelse.
- Legal requirements used as bedömningsgrund are cited (GR-001).
- States its scope/period explicitly — an undated rapport is incomplete.
