## Story

**As a** compliance officer reviewing an amendment assessment,
**I want** the AI to search the base law for the specific sections being amended (not just broad keywords),
**so that** the assessment grounds its analysis in the surrounding legal text of the changed paragraphs and is more precise.

## Context & Dependencies

### The Problem

The assessment playbook tells the AI to call `search_laws` to "Sök relevant lagtext för kontext" — but gives no guidance on *what* to search for. The AI generates broad keyword queries like "plan- och bygglagen bygglov detaljplan krav hantverkare" instead of targeted section lookups like "9 kap 80 § plan- och bygglagen."

This matters because the change-context block already injected into the system prompt provides:

- **Berörda paragrafer**: `Kap 9 § 80 (ändrad), Kap 9 § 97 (ändrad), …` — rendered by `loadChangeContext` in `lib/agent/system-prompt.ts` from the `SectionChange` table (Story 8.22).
- **Ändringstext**: the full published amendment text (the new lydelse) — also in the change-context block.
- **Bakgrund (proposition)**: why the law was changed (Story 8.24).

The AI has the section references but the playbook never tells it to feed them into `search_laws`.

### Important nuance — what `search_laws` is and isn't (read before implementing)

`search_laws` is **semantic / vector search** (`retrieveContext` over embedded document chunks), **not** an exact section lookup. Each chunk carries a `contextualHeader` and `path` (chapter/section), so a query like `"9 kap 80 § plan- och bygglagen"` *can* surface the right passage via header similarity — but it is not guaranteed, and the dev must confirm this in manual testing (Task 3).

Critically, the AI does **not** need `search_laws` to obtain the amended sections' *changed* text — that is already in the change-context block (Ändringstext) and via `get_change_details` (old/new lydelse per section). The genuine value of section-aware search is to retrieve the **base law's surrounding/consolidated provisions** — neighbouring subsections and related paragraphs the amendment text alone doesn't show — so the relevance analysis sits in proper context. The guidance must reflect this, not imply `search_laws` is how you fetch the amended text.

### Observed Behavior (SFS 2025:1079)

The AI searched:
1. "plan- och bygglagen bygglov lov förhandsbesked handläggning" — broad, generic
2. "Myndigheten för civilt försvar totalförsvar riksintresse bygglov" — better, but still keyword-based

Ideal searches would have referenced the affected sections, e.g. "9 kap 80 § plan- och bygglagen" / "9 kap 97 § plan- och bygglagen", to pull the surrounding base-law text for those paragraphs.

### How "Berörda paragrafer" is produced (read-only — for your understanding)

`loadChangeContext` (`lib/agent/system-prompt.ts:~373-392`) queries `SectionChange` rows for the amendment and renders:

```
- Berörda paragrafer: Kap 9 § 80 (ändrad), Kap 9 § 97 (ändrad)
```

Format: `Kap {chapter} § {section} ({changeTypeLabel})` where labels are `ändrad / upphävd / ny / omnumrerad`. `Kap {chapter} ` is omitted when the section has no chapter. This is the exact text the AI sees and can mine for its queries.

### `search_laws` behaviour (read-only — informs AC2/AC3 wording)

- Defined in `lib/agent/tools/search-laws.ts`. Input: `{ query: string, contentType?, limit? }`. It calls `retrieveContext(query, …)` — **semantic/embedding similarity**, not exact lookup. Results carry `contextualHeader`, `documentNumber`, `path`, `relevanceScore`, `snippet`, `citationKey`.
- Consequence for this story: a section-number query relies on the section path being embedded in the chunk's contextual header. It usually helps but is not deterministic — hence the manual-verification note in Task 4.

### `get_change_details` behaviour (read-only)

- Defined in `lib/agent/tools/get-change-details.ts`. Input: `{ changeEventId }` (the cuid from the change context, **not** an SFS number). Returns `affectedSections[]` with `chapter, section, changeType, description, oldText, newText` per paragraph (joined via `amendment_sfs → AmendmentDocument → SectionChange`). This is the authoritative source for old-vs-new wording — so AC2's distinction is grounded in real tool behaviour.

### Key Insight

The change-context block already contains the section references and the amended text. Avoid the original draft's framing that `search_laws` retrieves the amended text; it doesn't — `get_change_details` and the Ändringstext block do.
