# Story 5.5: Implement Usage Limits Per Tier

## Status

Ready for Development

> **Note:** Story 5.5 is now a **parent spec**. The actual implementation is split across **5.5a / 5.5b / 5.5c / 5.5d** (see "Sub-Stories" below). This document holds the calibrated v1 limits, the lib/usage architecture, and the cross-cutting decisions that all four sub-stories share.

## Story

**As a** product owner,
**I want** the platform to enforce per-tier limits on seats, storage, and AI usage,
**so that** workspace owners are guided to upgrade when they exceed what they're paying for, and unit economics stay positive even on heavy users.

## Context & Dependencies

- **Depends on:** Story 5.4 (Stripe subscription billing — shipped 2026-05-05). Limits read from `workspace.subscription_tier`, written by Stripe webhooks.
- **Builds on:** Story 14.27 (ChatUsageEvent) — already records per-turn token breakdowns + cost estimates. 5.5c aggregates from this signal rather than introducing a parallel counter.
- **Depended on by:** Story 5.6 (Add-On Purchase System, backlog) — extends limits via add-on Prices on the Stripe subscription.
- **Pricing source of truth:** `project_tier_pricing_v1.md` (memory, decided 2026-04-27). SEK plan, empirically calibrated against a 15-turn production cost test.

## Calibrated v1 Limits

| Dimension | Solo (499 SEK) | Team base (1 299 SEK, 3 users) | Team add-on seat (+300 SEK) | Enterprise (custom) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Users (seats)** | 1 | 3 | +1 per add-on | unlimited |
| **Employees (HR module)** | 5 | 20 | — | unlimited |
| **Storage** | 1 GB | 5 GB | — | 100 GB |
| **AI tokens / month (included)** | **3 000 000** | **9 000 000** | **+1 500 000** | unlimited |
| **AI display equivalent** | "≈ 100 AI-frågor" | "≈ 300 AI-frågor" | "+ 50 AI-frågor" | "Obegränsat" |
| **Soft warn threshold** | 80% (2.4M tokens) | 80% (7.2M) | applied at workspace total | — |
| **Hard cap** (option B for v1) | **2× included = 6M tokens** | **2× included = 18M tokens** | applied at workspace total | — |

### Margin reality check (Opus mean $0.084/turn ≈ $0.0028/1K tokens)[^pricing-methodology]

| | Cost @ 100% included | Revenue | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|
| Solo | $8.40 | $46 | **82%** |
| Team (base) | $25.20 | $120 | **79%** |
| Add-on seat | $4.20 | ~$28 | **85%** |

All above the 75% target. At the 2× hard cap, margin drops to 63-70% — tolerable for v1 runaway protection. Story 5.5d (Stripe metered overage) closes the gap by replacing the hard cap with pay-as-you-go billing once 30-60 days of real telemetry calibrates the per-token price.

[^pricing-methodology]: The `$0.084/turn` figure is derived from a 15-turn production cost test on 2026-04-27 against the Opus 4.6 projection (Sonnet 4.6 measured mean was $0.050/turn, Opus extrapolated to $0.084 using the 5/25 input/output rate ratio). Cache-read ratio 75.8%, outliers ~1 in 15 spike 4-5×, test was 100% `global` context with thinking disabled (real mix adds ~15-20% to mean). Source: `project_tier_pricing_v1.md`. Re-derive when AI model rates change or when ≥30 days of `ChatUsageEvent` data lands.

### Why these numbers + which decisions are deferrable

- **AI quota in tokens, not "queries":** "75 queries/month" reads as restrictive; "3 miljoner tokens (≈ 100 AI-frågor)" reads as substantive. Tokens also align with Stripe Meter billing for 5.5d and prevent abuse via long-context attachments inflating one "query" into a $1 turn.
- **Hard cap = 2× included quota** (option B): chosen over the original 110% cap. Avoids hostile UX during early adoption while still capping runaway abuse. Memory mandate: revisit after 30-60 days of `ChatUsageEvent` data lands.
- **Storage limits are positioning, not cost protection:** Supabase Pro storage is $0.021/GB/month with 100 GB included; even 1 000 active customers would cost <$25/month total. Limits exist so Team feels meaningfully more generous than Solo and to gate file-upload abuse.
- **Employee limits defined but not enforced:** HR module is not built yet. Limits live in `TIER_LIMITS` so the enforcement plumbing is already in place when the module ships — drop-in, no schema migration needed.

## Trial Behavior

A new workspace's trial gives **the user the full experience of the tier they pick at signup**, not a generic "trial" tier. Solo signup = Solo limits during trial. Team signup = Team limits during trial.

### Schema decision (recommended approach for v1)

The current `SubscriptionTier` enum has `{ TRIAL, SOLO, TEAM, ENTERPRISE }`. The cleanest long-term model is to **drop `TRIAL` from the tier enum** and use `status='trialing'` + `trial_ends_at` as the trial signal — tier IS what the user picked, status IS the lifecycle phase.

However, the existing codebase (BillingDashboard, Stripe webhook handlers, several UI surfaces) references `subscription_tier='TRIAL'`. A full migration is out of scope for 5.5.

**v1 approach (this story):**
- **Add column:** `Workspace.trial_picked_tier SubscriptionTier?` (nullable; only populated during trial)
- **Limit lookup:** `getLimits()` uses `trial_picked_tier ?? subscription_tier`. So a workspace with `subscription_tier='TRIAL'` and `trial_picked_tier='TEAM'` gets Team limits.
- **Set on signup:** The signup flow (Story 4.x — onboarding) writes `trial_picked_tier` when the user chooses a tier on the signup tier-picker.
- **Clear on conversion:** When trial converts to paid (`subscription_tier` flips to the picked tier), `trial_picked_tier` is set to NULL.

**Future cleanup (post-5.5d, separate story):** drop `TRIAL` from the enum, drop `trial_picked_tier` column, treat trial as a status state. Schedule when stable.

## Sub-Stories

Implementation is split into 4 focused sub-stories. Each is independently shippable; sequencing reflects user-priority + risk.

### 5.5a — Seat Enforcement

**Scope:** Block workspace member invitations + acceptance when adding the next user would exceed `TIER_LIMITS[tier].users`. UI: surface remaining seats in `/settings?tab=team` and on the invite modal. Employees marked deferred.

**Why first:** This is the user's primary stated concern ("Solo can't add a 2nd user"). Smallest blast radius — invite + accept flows only, no usage-table writes, no cron, no aggregation.

**Dimensions enforced:** `users`. Employees defined in `TIER_LIMITS` but enforcement no-op'd until HR module ships.

### 5.5b — Storage Enforcement

**Scope:** Pre-upload check on the file-upload endpoint. Blocks the upload (HTTP 413) if the new file would push the workspace over `TIER_LIMITS[tier].storageGB`. UI: storage meter on `/settings?tab=files` (or wherever Epic 17 surfaces file management).

**Why second:** Self-contained, doesn't depend on 5.5a. Storage usage is computable from the existing `File` table (`SELECT SUM(size_bytes) WHERE workspace_id = ?`).

**Dimensions enforced:** `storageGB`.

### 5.5c — AI Token Quota + Hard Cap (the meatiest sub-story)

**Scope:**

1. `WorkspaceUsage` Prisma model (denormalized counter, updated each turn — see schema below)
2. `lib/usage/limits.ts` (the `TIER_LIMITS` const + helpers)
3. `lib/usage/check.ts` (`assertWithinTokenQuota` wrapper called by `app/api/chat/route.ts` before `streamText`)
4. Token counter increment inside the existing `onFinish` callback (next to the `ChatUsageEvent` write)
5. Hard cap at 2× included quota — block with HTTP 402 + `code: 'AI_TOKEN_QUOTA_EXCEEDED'`
6. Soft warn at 80% — non-blocking; surfaces in chat response metadata for UI
7. **Billing-cycle-anchored reset** — Pattern A. Counter resets when each workspace's Stripe `current_period_end` advances (via `customer.subscription.updated` webhook). Trial workspaces use `workspace.created_at` as the initial `period_started_at`; trial→paid conversion advances `current_period_end` from NULL → future, triggering the first reset. Defensive weekly safety cron (`/api/cron/reset-usage-safety`) catches stale rows from missed webhooks.
8. Usage dashboard widget on `/settings?tab=billing` showing "X / 3M tokens använda denna månad (≈ Y AI-frågor)"

**Why third:** Needs new infra (`WorkspaceUsage` model, cron, dashboard widget). Independently testable but touches the chat hot path — needs careful telemetry to make sure quota check doesn't tank chat latency.

**Dimensions enforced:** `aiTokensPerMonth`.

### 5.5d — Stripe Metered Overage (DEFERRED)

**Scope:** Replace 5.5c's hard cap with Stripe Meter + per-turn `billing.meterEvents.create`. Users above the included quota pay per-token instead of being blocked. Customer-facing dashboard shows projected month-end overage cost.

**Why deferred:** Per `project_tier_pricing_v1.md`, the per-token price needs to be calibrated against 30-60 days of real-user telemetry — we don't have enough production data to set it correctly today. Setting it too low = under-billed heavy users. Setting it too high = sticker shock + churn. Ship 5.5c hard caps first, watch the `ChatUsageEvent` distribution, then ship 5.5d with calibrated overage pricing.

**Pre-conditions for unblocking:** ≥ 30 days of `ChatUsageEvent` data spanning ≥ 20 active workspaces, p50/p90/p99 cost-per-turn known, monthly overage volume estimable.

## Acceptance Criteria (Story 5.5 parent)

These ACs apply to the **family** of 5.5 work — individual sub-stories have their own granular ACs.

1. **Calibrated limits live in `lib/usage/limits.ts`** with the exact values in the table above. Single source of truth — no hardcoded limits anywhere else.
2. **`getLimits(workspace)` resolves trial-picked-tier correctly**: a workspace with `subscription_tier='TRIAL'` and `trial_picked_tier='TEAM'` returns Team limits.
3. **Seat enforcement (5.5a)** prevents adding users beyond `TIER_LIMITS[tier].users`. Both invite-create and invite-accept paths gated.
4. **Storage enforcement (5.5b)** rejects uploads that would exceed `TIER_LIMITS[tier].storageGB` with a clear Swedish error.
5. **AI token tracking (5.5c)** writes to `WorkspaceUsage.tokens_used_this_period` in the same transaction as `ChatUsageEvent`. No drift.
6. **Soft warn at 80%** of included quota — surfaces in chat response for UI; does NOT block.
7. **Hard cap at 2× included quota** (option B for v1): chat endpoint returns HTTP 402 with `code: 'AI_TOKEN_QUOTA_EXCEEDED'`, `message` (Swedish), `usage`, `limit`, `tier`.
8. **Billing-cycle-anchored reset (Pattern A):** `tokens_used_this_period` is reset to 0 when a workspace's Stripe `current_period_end` advances. Implemented in the `customer.subscription.updated` webhook handler at `app/api/webhooks/stripe/route.ts`. **Trial workspaces:** `WorkspaceUsage.period_started_at = workspace.created_at` at trial start; conversion to paid (first Stripe Subscription) advances `current_period_end` from NULL → future date and triggers the first reset — the trial counts as the first usage period and gets zeroed at conversion. **Plan upgrade/downgrade mid-period does NOT reset** — counter continues until the next anniversary. **Safety cron** at `/api/cron/reset-usage-safety` runs weekly (`0 4 * * 0`); resets any `WorkspaceUsage` rows where `period_started_at < NOW() - INTERVAL '35 days'` to catch missed webhooks. Idempotent.
9. **Employee limits defined in `TIER_LIMITS` but NOT enforced** in any code path. Marked clearly as "deferred — HR module not built". Deferred dimension does not break any test.
10. **Usage widget on `/settings?tab=billing`** shows current period usage vs limit for tokens AND seats AND storage. Token display includes the "≈ X AI-frågor" estimate.
11. **Enterprise tier returns `null` for all numeric limits** and `getLimits()` callers correctly interpret null as unlimited (no false-block).
12. **Test coverage** per sub-story (see 5.5a/b/c/d) — minimum unit tests for limit computation, integration tests for enforcement on the chat + invite + upload paths.

## Dev Notes

### `lib/usage/limits.ts` — single source of truth

```typescript
import type { SubscriptionTier, Workspace } from '@prisma/client'

export interface UsageLimits {
  users: number | null              // null = unlimited
  employees: number | null          // defined but NOT enforced in v1 (HR module not built)
  aiTokensPerMonth: number | null   // soft warn at 80%, hard cap at 2× this value
  storageGB: number | null
}

// Calibrated v1 limits — see Story 5.5 for empirical basis (project_tier_pricing_v1.md)
// Add-on seats are NOT a separate tier; the Team add-on adds +1 user, +1.5M tokens
// to the workspace's effective limits. This delta is computed at lookup time
// from the workspace's active Stripe subscription items (see getEffectiveLimits below).
export const TIER_LIMITS: Record<SubscriptionTier, UsageLimits> = {
  TRIAL: {
    // TRIAL is a fallback only — getLimits() resolves trial_picked_tier first.
    // If somehow a workspace lands here (no picked tier set), Solo limits apply.
    users: 1,
    employees: 5,
    aiTokensPerMonth: 3_000_000,
    storageGB: 1,
  },
  SOLO: {
    users: 1,
    employees: 5,
    aiTokensPerMonth: 3_000_000,
    storageGB: 1,
  },
  TEAM: {
    users: 3,
    employees: 20,
    aiTokensPerMonth: 9_000_000,
    storageGB: 5,
  },
  ENTERPRISE: {
    users: null,
    employees: null,
    aiTokensPerMonth: null,
    storageGB: 100,
  },
}

// Add-on contribution per Stripe Subscription Item beyond the base tier
export const TEAM_ADDON_PER_SEAT = {
  users: 1,
  aiTokensPerMonth: 1_500_000,
} as const

/**
 * Resolve the effective limits for a workspace. Handles trial-picked-tier
 * fallback and Team add-on seat math.
 */
export function getEffectiveLimits(
  workspace: Pick<Workspace, 'subscription_tier' | 'trial_picked_tier'>,
  addonSeatCount: number = 0
): UsageLimits {
  const tier = workspace.trial_picked_tier ?? workspace.subscription_tier
  const base = TIER_LIMITS[tier]

  // Add-ons only apply to TEAM (Solo/Enterprise have their own structure)
  if (tier !== 'TEAM' || addonSeatCount === 0) return base

  return {
    users: base.users === null ? null : base.users + addonSeatCount * TEAM_ADDON_PER_SEAT.users,
    employees: base.employees,
    aiTokensPerMonth:
      base.aiTokensPerMonth === null
        ? null
        : base.aiTokensPerMonth + addonSeatCount * TEAM_ADDON_PER_SEAT.aiTokensPerMonth,
    storageGB: base.storageGB,
  }
}

export function isUnlimited(limit: number | null): limit is null {
  return limit === null
}

// Token-based AI quota helpers
export const AI_SOFT_WARN_THRESHOLD = 0.80   // warn at 80% of included
export const AI_HARD_CAP_MULTIPLIER = 2.0    // block at 2× included (option B for v1)

export function tokensSoftWarn(limit: number | null): number | null {
  return isUnlimited(limit) ? null : Math.floor(limit * AI_SOFT_WARN_THRESHOLD)
}

export function tokensHardCap(limit: number | null): number | null {
  return isUnlimited(limit) ? null : Math.floor(limit * AI_HARD_CAP_MULTIPLIER)
}

// UI helper — display "≈ X AI-frågor" alongside token count.
// Based on production avg of ~30K tokens per typical turn (Opus, mixed context).
export function tokensToApproxQueries(tokens: number): number {
  return Math.round(tokens / 30_000)
}
```

### `WorkspaceUsage` Prisma model (5.5c)

```prisma
model WorkspaceUsage {
  id                       String   @id @default(uuid())
  workspace_id             String   @unique
  // AI tokens consumed in the current billing period.
  // Reset is billing-cycle-anchored (Pattern A): the customer.subscription.updated
  // webhook zeroes this when current_period_end advances. Trial workspaces start
  // with period_started_at = workspace.created_at; conversion to paid resets.
  // A weekly safety cron catches stale rows (period_started_at > 35 days old).
  // Aggregates input + output + cache_read + cache_write from ChatUsageEvent.
  tokens_used_this_period  BigInt   @default(0)
  storage_bytes            BigInt   @default(0)  // recomputed on file upload/delete
  period_started_at        DateTime @default(now())
  updated_at               DateTime @updatedAt

  workspace Workspace @relation(fields: [workspace_id], references: [id], onDelete: Cascade)

  @@map("workspace_usage")
}
```

Note: `BigInt` for tokens — at 18M tokens/mo for Team and potential heavy outliers, regular `Int` (max ~2.1B) is plenty headroom but `BigInt` is the safer choice as overage limits relax in 5.5d.

### Quota check — synchronous, before `streamText`

```typescript
// lib/usage/check.ts
import { prisma } from '@/lib/prisma'
import {
  getEffectiveLimits,
  isUnlimited,
  tokensSoftWarn,
  tokensHardCap,
  tokensToApproxQueries,
} from './limits'

export class TokenQuotaExceededError extends Error {
  constructor(
    public usage: number,
    public limit: number,
    public hardCap: number,
    public tier: string
  ) {
    super(`AI token quota exceeded: ${usage}/${hardCap} (tier: ${tier})`)
  }
}

export async function assertWithinTokenQuota(workspaceId: string): Promise<{
  warning?: { usage: number; limit: number; approxQueries: number }
}> {
  const [workspace, usage, addonSeats] = await Promise.all([
    prisma.workspace.findUniqueOrThrow({
      where: { id: workspaceId },
      select: { subscription_tier: true, trial_picked_tier: true },
    }),
    prisma.workspaceUsage.findUnique({ where: { workspace_id: workspaceId } }),
    countActiveAddonSeats(workspaceId), // sums Stripe Subscription Items beyond base
  ])

  const limits = getEffectiveLimits(workspace, addonSeats)
  if (isUnlimited(limits.aiTokensPerMonth)) return {}

  const consumed = Number(usage?.tokens_used_this_period ?? 0n)
  const limit = limits.aiTokensPerMonth
  const hardCap = tokensHardCap(limit)!

  if (consumed >= hardCap) {
    throw new TokenQuotaExceededError(
      consumed,
      limit,
      hardCap,
      workspace.trial_picked_tier ?? workspace.subscription_tier
    )
  }

  const softWarn = tokensSoftWarn(limit)!
  if (consumed >= softWarn) {
    return {
      warning: {
        usage: consumed,
        limit,
        approxQueries: tokensToApproxQueries(limit - consumed),
      },
    }
  }

  return {}
}
```

The `app/api/chat/route.ts` calls `assertWithinTokenQuota` BEFORE `streamText` and surfaces `warning` in the response stream metadata. The token counter increment happens in the existing `onFinish` callback in the same transaction as the `ChatUsageEvent` write.

### Reset trigger — Pattern A (per-customer billing-cycle-anchored)

**Primary mechanism: webhook-driven reset.** The Stripe `customer.subscription.updated` event fires whenever a workspace's `current_period_end` advances — i.e., on every billing anniversary AND on the first conversion from trial → paid (NULL → future date). The existing webhook handler (`app/api/webhooks/stripe/route.ts`, Story 5.4) already writes `current_period_end` to the workspace; extend it to ALSO reset the usage counter when the period advances:

```typescript
// app/api/webhooks/stripe/route.ts (extension of existing handler at line 95)
case 'customer.subscription.updated': {
  const sub = event.data.object as Stripe.Subscription
  const workspaceId = sub.metadata.workspaceId
  if (!workspaceId) break

  // Stripe API 2024-04-10+ moved current_period_end and current_period_start
  // ONTO the SubscriptionItem. Older API versions keep them on the subscription
  // itself. The existing handler reads from item-first with subscription
  // fallback (lines 106-112) — REUSE that pattern for both fields.
  const item = sub.items.data[0] as
    | (Stripe.SubscriptionItem & {
        current_period_end?: number
        current_period_start?: number
      })
    | undefined
  const periodEndUnix =
    item?.current_period_end ??
    (sub as unknown as { current_period_end?: number }).current_period_end
  const periodStartUnix =
    item?.current_period_start ??
    (sub as unknown as { current_period_start?: number }).current_period_start

  const newPeriodEnd = periodEndUnix ? new Date(periodEndUnix * 1000) : null
  const newPeriodStart = periodStartUnix
    ? new Date(periodStartUnix * 1000)
    : null

  const existing = await prisma.workspace.findUnique({
    where: { id: workspaceId },
    select: { current_period_end: true },
  })

  // Period advanced → new billing cycle → reset usage counter.
  // NULL → future also counts (first conversion from trial).
  // Both newPeriodEnd === null AND existing.current_period_end === null
  // means we received a subscription event with no period info yet (rare,
  // possibly an incomplete-subscription state) — skip the reset.
  const periodAdvanced =
    !!newPeriodEnd &&
    (!existing?.current_period_end || newPeriodEnd > existing.current_period_end)

  await prisma.$transaction([
    prisma.workspace.update({
      where: { id: workspaceId },
      data: { current_period_end: newPeriodEnd, /* …other 5.4 fields */ },
    }),
    ...(periodAdvanced && newPeriodStart
      ? [
          prisma.workspaceUsage.upsert({
            where: { workspace_id: workspaceId },
            update: {
              tokens_used_this_period: 0n,
              period_started_at: newPeriodStart,
            },
            create: {
              workspace_id: workspaceId,
              tokens_used_this_period: 0n,
              period_started_at: newPeriodStart,
            },
          }),
        ]
      : []),
  ])
}
```

**Trial workspaces** start with `WorkspaceUsage.period_started_at = workspace.created_at` (set when the row is first created on the first chat turn). The trial period IS the first usage period — usage accumulates throughout the trial. When the user converts (clicks Välj nivå → Stripe Checkout completes), `customer.subscription.updated` fires with `current_period_end` going from NULL → future date. The handler above detects the advance and resets the counter — **the trial-end conversion event resets cleanly without special-casing**.

**Defensive safety cron.** A weekly cron catches workspaces whose webhook reset failed (network blip, Stripe outage, deploy race, etc.):

```typescript
// app/api/cron/reset-usage-safety/route.ts
import { NextResponse } from 'next/server'
import { prisma } from '@/lib/prisma'

export const dynamic = 'force-dynamic'
export const maxDuration = 60

export async function GET(request: Request) {
  const authHeader = request.headers.get('authorization')
  if (
    process.env.NODE_ENV !== 'development' &&
    authHeader !== `Bearer ${process.env.CRON_SECRET}`
  ) {
    return NextResponse.json({ error: 'Unauthorized' }, { status: 401 })
  }

  const cutoff = new Date(Date.now() - 35 * 24 * 60 * 60 * 1000)
  const result = await prisma.workspaceUsage.updateMany({
    where: { period_started_at: { lt: cutoff } },
    data: { tokens_used_this_period: 0n, period_started_at: new Date() },
  })

  console.log(`[RESET-USAGE-SAFETY] Reset ${result.count} stale rows`)
  return NextResponse.json({ success: true, reset: result.count })
}
```

`vercel.json`:
```json
{ "path": "/api/cron/reset-usage-safety", "schedule": "0 4 * * 0" }
```

Schedule is Sunday 04:00 UTC (= ~05:00 SE winter / 06:00 SE summer) — low-traffic time. 35-day threshold = monthly + 5 days grace, so it only catches genuinely-stuck rows, never overlaps with an in-flight billing cycle. This is a backstop, not the primary mechanism — most workspaces never hit the safety cron at all.

### Why this is correct for Laglig

- **Margin protection:** revenue books on Stripe anniversary; costs reset on the same date. No exploit window where a heavy mid-month user gets a calendar-month "free reset" plus their fresh next-month quota.
- **User-predictable:** "Your quota resets on your billing date" matches what users intuitively expect from a paid subscription.
- **Stripe-native:** the reset signal IS the billing event. No translation layer between billing and quota windows.
- **Forward-compatible with 5.5d:** Stripe metered billing windows are also per-anniversary. Aligning included quotas to the same window means 5.5d's overage logic flips on without recalibrating period boundaries.

### Why aggregate counter + ChatUsageEvent both, not just one

`ChatUsageEvent` is the authoritative log (Story 14.27 — used for cost telemetry, admin analytics, future Stripe metered billing). `WorkspaceUsage.tokens_used_this_period` is a denormalized counter for O(1) quota check on the chat hot path. They MUST stay in sync — both written in the same `onFinish` transaction. A drift-detection cron is a candidate for 5.5d but not required for v1.

### Quota check failure response shape

When hard cap is hit, `app/api/chat/route.ts` returns:

```json
{
  "error": "AI-tokenkvot uppnådd",
  "message": "Du har använt 6 100 000 av 6 000 000 tokens (200%) denna månad. Uppgradera planen för att fortsätta.",
```
