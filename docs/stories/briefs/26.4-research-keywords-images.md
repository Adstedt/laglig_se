# Research Input — Story 26.4: Fem content briefs för Laglig.se marketing pages

Prepared for: PO + founder (final copywriting + image generation). All user-facing strings are in Swedish; commentary in English. Sources: keyword research pass, screenshot spec pass, Nano Banana prompt pass (June 2026).

---

## Cross-page summary

**Keyword themes.** Two clusters: (1) industry pages target "vilka lagar gäller för X / laglista X" — the moment an SME maps its legal obligations, which is exactly the laglista buyer; (2) feature pages target the ISO-ecosystem vocabulary ("lagefterlevnadskontroll") and the AI-modified variant of the proven head term ("AI-lagbevakning"). Across all five pages: do **not** chase myndighet-owned head terms (Arbetsmiljöverket, Livsmedelsverket, IMY, MCF/PTS, Boverket own arbetsmiljöplan/HACCP/GDPR/NIS2/PBL) — use them as supporting H2/FAQ content under a winnable commercial primary.

**Competitor gap (consistent across pages).** The laglista vendors (Notisum/Karnov, Aptor, Lagpunkten, laglistan.se, Ecowise, Certway) rank only on generic product pages skewed to miljö/arbetsmiljö; only Lagpunkten has a single bransch page (bygg). Nobody combines (a) a comprehensive Swedish "hela lagkartan för er bransch" guide, (b) the actual product workflow shown (kravpunkter, kontroller, lagändringsbevakning), and (c) freshness on 2025/2026 regulatory changes. On the AI side, only Efterleva.se (notification newsletter) is AI-native — no one markets an agent that acts *inside* a laglista. That guide→product page is the play on every brief.

**Image strategy.** All pages: product screenshots carry the argument (grouped laglista as hero on bransch pages; kontroll-cycle table as hero on kontroller; Lexa-in-context as hero on ai-agent). People photos are secondary proof blocks — candid Swedish documentary style, warm-neutral grade tuned to the cream `#faf9f6` page background, no readable text/logos anywhere, no camera awareness, and **flawless depicted compliance** (PPE, hygiene, safety) since the page topics are exactly those laws. The ai-agent page should default to screenshots-only; its single human image is optional and droppable.

## The guide→product bridge (structural rule, not aspiration)

**Every editorial section on a bransch page must hand off to the product surface that solves it** (Alexander, 2026-06-10). A page that explains lagkrav without showing the laglista is a Wikipedia article wearing our domain; a page that's all product is an ad nobody ranks for. The narrative arc per bransch page:

1. **Hero** — buyer-mental-model headline + CTA + the grouped laglista screenshot (the product IS the answer to the search query)
2. **Per H2 cluster** — editorial explanation of the lagkrav area → `<SplitFeature>` row showing the exact product surface that operationalizes it:
   - "Vilka lagkrav gäller…" → grouped laglista (per-industry groups)
   - Arbetsmiljö/AFS section → kravpunkter checklist in the law modal ("från paragraf till checklista")
   - "Nya lagar 2026" section → Ändringar tab / lagändringsbevakning ("ni behöver inte läsa SFS själva")
3. **Mid-page** — `<OrgCheckCta>` ("Se vilka regler som gäller just er — på 30 sekunder") right after the reader has absorbed how much law applies to them: peak problem-awareness = peak conversion moment
4. **`<CatalogLawList>`** — the laws named in the editorial, linked live into the catalog (proof of depth no competitor can fake)
5. **FAQ → footer CTA band** — objection handling into the final conversion ask

Feature pages follow the same rule inverted: each capability claim sits next to the screenshot proving it, with the editorial explaining the lagkrav context that makes the capability matter.

**This becomes an explicit acceptance criterion in Story 26.4:** no editorial H2 section without a product hand-off in or directly after it. The MDX template supports it natively (`<SplitFeature>` rows interleave with prose).
