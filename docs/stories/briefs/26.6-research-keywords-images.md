## The product→guide bridge (the 26.4 rule, inverted for feature pages)

> Carried from the 26.4/26.5 briefs with the PO's clarified ruling: **at least one explicit product sentence per editorial H2, and one `<SplitFeature>` per H2 cluster** (not per H2).

On bransch pages the editorial explains the lagkrav and hands off to the product. **Feature pages read the same rule inverted: each capability claim sits next to the screenshot proving it, with the editorial explaining the lagkrav context that makes the capability matter.** A feature page that lists capabilities without lagkrav context is an ad nobody ranks for; one that only explains the law is a Wikipedia article wearing our domain. Narrative arc per feature page:

1. **Hero** — capability headline in the buyer's vocabulary + CTA + the hero screenshot (the UI IS the claim)
2. **Per H2 cluster** — capability claim → screenshot proving it → editorial paragraph on the lagkrav/revision context that makes it matter (ISO 6.1.3/9.1.2, IVO/tillsyn, miljöbalkens egenkontroll)
3. **Mid-page** — `<OrgCheckCta>` after peak problem-awareness
4. **`<CatalogLawList>`** — the laws named in the editorial, linked live (only catalog-confirmed ACTIVE numbers below)
5. **FAQ → footer CTA band**

**Hard guard (Alexander, 2026-06-11, binding):** kravpunkten definierar att en kontroll/rutin ska finnas och följas (kan peka på styrdokument/underlag); den dagliga ronden körs i verksamhetens egna rutiner — **Laglig är INTE ett dagligt rond-avprickningsverktyg**; kontrollcyklerna reviderar kravpunkten (att kontrollerna körs); det mobila on-site-fallet är bevisdokumentation (foto länkat som bevis på kravpunkten). No H2, FAQ or copy angle on any of these five pages may frame kravpunkter or uppgifter as daily check-off/rond tools. Additionally, **no copy may claim auto-recurring kontrollcykler** (cycles are one-time manual creations — confirmed in code on three independent staging passes).
