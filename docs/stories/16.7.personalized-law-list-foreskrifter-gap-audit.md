# Story 16.7: Personalized law-list generation — agency föreskrifter + gap-audit pipeline

## Story

**As a** new customer onboarding to laglig.se (especially in a regulated sector such as a school),
**I want** my auto-generated law list to be deeply personalized — derived from my company profile and including the *myndighetsföreskrifter* (agency regulations) that actually govern my operations, not just a template echo,
**so that** the list is a trustworthy high-recall draft I can review and trim, rather than a generic skeleton that misses the regulations my business is legally bound by.

### What prompted this (the two defects)

The generation for Nordvik Utbildning AB (friskola, SNI 85.20) produced a ~30-item list that:

1. **Ignored agency regulations entirely.** Zero SKOLFS (Skolverket föreskrifter) and zero other myndighetsföreskrifter — despite these being the *operative* obligations for a school. The corpus contained them and `search_laws` could reach them; the generation prompt simply never asked.
2. **Read as a template echo.** It "essentially only took from our templates more or less" — the prompt was template-anchored (search was "gaps only", capped, and told not to re-search template hits), and never instructed the model to mine `sni_code` / `business_description` to drive personalized research.

Root cause was entirely in the generation prompt and the absence of a verification pass — not the data model or tools. `LegalDocument` already stores `AGENCY_REGULATION` rows (Story 9.5/9.7), and `retrieveContext` already returns them unless a content-type filter excludes them.

## Acceptance Criteria

### A. Phase A — personalized build (committed in `d1844db8`)

1. **`SYSTEM_PROMPT`** instructs the model to derive **which agencies regulate this company** from `sni_code`, `industry_label`, `business_description`, and `activity_flags` (returned by `get_company_context`) — not from the industry label alone.
2. **A dedicated `AGENCY_REGULATION` search pass is mandatory.** The prompt requires targeted `search_laws` calls with `contentType: "AGENCY_REGULATION"` so föreskrifter are not crowded out of rerank by SFS hits.
3. **Templates are reframed as a floor, not the answer** ("ett golv, inte facit"); the throttles that caused the template-echo ("search gaps only", "max 8-10 searches", "don't re-search template hits") are removed.
4. **Default model is `claude-opus-4-8`** (override via `LAW_LIST_GENERATION_MODEL`), with **adaptive thinking at `high` effort** (`thinking:{type:'adaptive',display:'summarized'}, effort:'high'`). Previously thinking was OFF (no `thinking` block ⇒ disabled on these models), starving a reasoning-heavy task.
5. **`search_laws` tool description** advertises föreskrifter (SKOLFS/LIVSFS/HSLF-FS/BFS…) and the `contentType:"AGENCY_REGULATION"` filter, for discoverability.
6. **`maxDuration` on `POST /api/workspace/generate-law-list` raised 300 → 600s** (Vercel Fluid Compute; ceiling 800s), so Opus-with-thinking runs are not killed mid-flight.

### B. Phase B — independent gap-audit pipeline (uncommitted)

7. After Phase A, an **independent gap audit** runs in a **fresh context** that sees only `(company profile + the finished list)` and hunts for *absent whole regimes* — decorrelating its errors from the build pass (a model that anchored on "school" carries that blind spot into in-loop self-critique).
8. **The audit is a fixed two-stage pipeline, NOT open-ended loop-until-dry:**
   - **Stage 1 — discover:** broad role/regime checklist gap hunt (`GAP_AUDIT_SYSTEM_PROMPT`, ≤16 steps).
   - **Stage 2 — consolidate:** a *narrow* structural pass (`CONSOLIDATION_SYSTEM_PROMPT`, ≤8 steps) that only pairs föreskrifter with their parent statute (and vice-versa) and otherwise adds nothing.
   - The pipeline **stops early** if a stage adds no new items.
9. **Role/regime checklist** (the recall mechanism) enumerates obligations by *what the company is/does*, not its industry: employer; premises/verksamhet (miljöbalken + egenkontroll + **avfall**); personuppgifter; arbetar med barn; **bedriver hälso-/sjukvård (elevhälsa → HSL/patientsäkerhet/patientdata)**; privat välfärdsaktör (meddelarskydd); livsmedel; kemikalier/avfall.
10. **Bidirectional "rätt nivå" rule:** a framework law without its föreskrift, OR föreskrifter without their parent statute, is a gap. The consolidate stage exists specifically to anchor the parent statutes that the discover stage's own additions orphan (e.g. adding miljö-förordningar exposes that Miljöbalken 1998:808 is absent).
11. **Effort tiering:** Phase A = `high`; both audit stages = `medium`. Rationale: the audit is checklist-guided, and known regimes are *encoded in the checklist* rather than re-inferred — so the top tiers buy little and overrun the 600s budget (xhigh discovery measured at 703s).
12. **Kill switch:** `LAW_LIST_GAP_AUDIT=false` skips Phase B entirely.
13. **`runGapAudit(workspaceId, userId)` is exported** so it can run standalone against an existing list (validation harness) without a full Phase A rebuild.

### C. Observability

14. **`GenerateLawListResult.steps`** records `{ generation, audit, auditPasses }` so "did we hit `stepCountIs`?" and "how many audit passes ran?" are observable numbers, not inferences from the deduped progress trace.
15. **The route's Sentry `generation_result` context** logs `generationSteps`, `auditSteps`, `auditPasses` (alongside the existing token/cost fields).

### D. Swedish-language quality (model- and user-facing prompts)

16. Coined/calque terms removed from the audit prompts: **"luckjakt" → "genomgång av luckor"**, **"moderlag" → "den lag/förordning föreskriften meddelats med stöd av" / "överordnad författning"**, and the user-facing progress label **"Förankrar ram- och föreskrifter" → "Kopplar ihop lagar och föreskrifter"**.

### E. Dev/test ergonomics

17. **`scripts/reset-school-law-list.ts`** clears the seeded school test workspace's list and re-arms the first-run modal (status=null, `first_run_dismissed_at`=null, `created_at`=now for the 24h fresh-window guard).

### Key decisions baked into this change

- **Separate audit phase, not in-loop self-review.** Self-critique inside the build context shares the build's anchoring → correlated errors. A fresh pass over the finished list catches whole missing regimes (validated: it independently recovered miljöbalken/hälsoskydd, barnkonventionen, meddelarskydd, and an elevhälsa→HSL dimension the author missed).
- **Discover → consolidate, NOT loop-until-dry.** On an additive task, "stop when nothing's added" rewards finding ever-more-marginal items (precision decay). The second stage is given a *different, narrow* mandate and capped at 2 — so unbounded padding is structurally impossible.
- **Encode regimes in the checklist; don't pay effort to re-infer them.** xhigh discovery (703s, $5.80) reached 7/7 target regimes including avfall + elevhälsa — but adding those two as plain checklist text made **medium** discovery reach the same 7/7 deterministically. Effort tiers are reserved for genuinely novel cases.
- **High build / medium audits** is the budget-fit point (~512–640s on a quiet box) that preserves quality.
- **Quality target = high-recall draft for user audit, not a "perfect" list.** Items land `Ej granskad`; the user trims by applicability. For a *reviewed* list the asymmetry favors recall (a missing law is invisible and dangerous; an extra one is a 2-second delete), so generation leans inclusive and `businessContext` per item is the trim-enabler. This makes "did it match my mental gold list exactly" the wrong success metric.

### Out of scope / known gaps (follow-ups)

- **No objective eval.** Quality was assessed by domain eyeballing. The highest-value follow-up is a **gold-standard eval harness** (expert-reviewed lists per company archetype, scored recall-weighted on material laws) so quality is measurable, not vibes.
- **Food operative föreskrifter** (EU 852/2004 + LIVSFS) — framework laws present, operative hygiene rules still thin in some runs.
- **Smittskyddslag (2004:168)** and **orosanmälan / SoL 14 kap** — plausible school gaps not consistently captured.
- **AFS 2023:2 chapter-row granularity** inflates the count; acceptable under the draft-for-audit model (user trims), not "fixed" at generation time.
- **`businessContext` parity** between Phase A and audit-added items — recommended cheap verification (the audit-added items must carry context as strong as the build's, since that's the trim-enabler).

- Final shippable config: **A=high · discover=medium · consolidate=medium · checklist-enriched**, ~$4–5.50, ~512–640s, under the 600s budget on a quiet box.
