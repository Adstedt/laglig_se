# Story 16.5: Business Context Voice + Tiered Hedging for AI-Generated Law Lists

## Status

Draft

## Dependencies

- **Story 16.4** (headless law list generation skill) — provides `lib/agent/skills/generate-law-list.ts` and the `SYSTEM_PROMPT` that this story modifies. Done.
- **Schema:** `LawListItemSource` enum (`ONBOARDING | MANUAL | IMPORT`) already exists in `prisma/schema.prisma` line 723–727. No migration required by this story.

## Story

**As a** newly onboarded user reading the AI-generated business_context on each law in my list,
**I want** the text to address my company directly in a clearly-advisory voice with honest hedging when the suggestion is inferred rather than universal,
**so that** I can quickly tell which laws are definitively applicable vs. need my review, the language doesn't pretend the company itself authored unverified statements, and Laglig is legally protected when an AI-suggested law turns out not to apply.

## Background & Rationale

The current prompt at `lib/agent/skills/generate-law-list.ts:80–128` does not specify a voice rule. As a result, the model improvises and writes business_context using the literal company name in third person ("Alloffice Nordic har…"), which:

1. **Reads like an external analyst report**, not customer-facing advice.
2. **Repeats the company name 50–80 times** across a single list — visually noisy.
3. **Misleads on confidence:** sector-template-derived laws (high confidence) and search-inferred laws (low confidence) get the same definitive language. Customers cannot tell which suggestions to scrutinize.
4. **Creates legal exposure** when an AI-suggested law turns out to be inapplicable — definitive verbs ("omfattas av", "berör", "är tillämplig") read as advice rather than suggestion.
5. **Is inconsistent with the prompt's own example** at line 117, which uses "Ni omfattas… era köksprocesser… ert systematiska arbetsmiljöarbete" (correct second-person plural advisory voice). The example exists but is not reinforced by an explicit rule.

This story adds an explicit voice rule (Ni / second-person plural) and a tiered-hedging rule (verb strength scaled to derivation confidence), plus a mandatory verification close. Imported lists are unaffected because they do not pass through this skill.

**Out of scope (intentional — escalate to Epic if/when prioritised):**

- New `confidence` field on `LawListItem` schema
- UI badge for un-reviewed AI suggestions ("AI-förslag — granska")
- Onboarding "Granska era förslag" review step
- Disclaimer modal / Terms of Service language (Story 3.12 territory)
- Backfill of existing AI-generated business_context on already-onboarded workspaces

These are tracked in **Follow-Ups** at the bottom of this file.

## Acceptance Criteria

### Voice

1. The `SYSTEM_PROMPT` in `lib/agent/skills/generate-law-list.ts` MUST include an explicit **Skrivton** (voice) rule directing the model to write all `businessContext` in second-person plural Swedish ("Ni", "er", "era", "ert"). The rule MUST forbid: (a) using the company name in the body text, (b) first-person plural ("Vi", "vår"), (c) third-person ("Företaget", "Bolaget"). Rationale must be inline so future prompt edits preserve the constraint.

2. The single existing example at line 117 MUST be expanded into **three examples**, one per derivation tier (universal, template-derived, search-inferred — see AC 3), all using the "Ni" voice. The previous one-example pattern is insufficient; the model needs to see the *gradient* of certainty.

### Tiered Hedging

3. The prompt MUST instruct the model to choose verb strength per the law's derivation tier:

   - **Universal** (laws every Swedish aktiebolag is bound by — note: the seven `UNIVERSAL_AB_LAWS` are pre-seeded outside the LLM path, but the model may legitimately add more universal laws through templates/search): definitive language permitted — "Som aktiebolag omfattas Ni av…", "Lagen reglerar…".
   - **Template-derived** (returned by `get_template_laws` for an area matched to the company profile): judgment language required — "Med [N] anställda inom [bransch] bedöms Ni omfattas av…", "Lagen kan beröra era…".
   - **Search-inferred** (returned by `search_laws` based on activity flags or industry niche): conditional language required — "Beroende på hur Ni hanterar [X] kan denna lag vara tillämplig…", "Förutsatt att…".

4. The prompt MUST list the approved hedge keywords explicitly: **bedöms, sannolikt, kan beröra, beroende på, förutsatt att, vid förekomst av**. The model should pick from this list rather than inventing variants — keeps tone consistent across 50–80 items.

### Verification Close

5. Every `businessContext` for **template-derived** and **search-inferred** tiers MUST end with a verification cue addressed to the reader. Approved phrasings (model picks one, may rotate to avoid monotony):
   - "Granska om beskrivningen stämmer för er verksamhet."
   - "Verifiera tillämpligheten innan ni planerar åtgärder."
   - "Bekräfta att lagen berör er innan ni går vidare."

   Universal-tier items do NOT require the verification close (they ARE applicable, no judgment needed).

### Pre-Seeded Universal Laws

6. The seven `UNIVERSAL_AB_LAWS` business_context strings at `lib/agent/skills/generate-law-list.ts:134–181` MUST be left in their current neutral third-person voice ("Aktiebolagslagen reglerar…", "Bokföringslagen kräver…"). These are boilerplate descriptions of the law itself, not company-specific advice. Rewriting them into "Ni"-voice would add churn for low value and break consistency with how laws are described elsewhere (e.g., `LegalDocument.summary`). The decision is intentional — document it in a one-line code comment above the array.

### Length & Structure (unchanged from 16.4)

7. `businessContext` remains 2–3 sentences covering: (1) why this law applies, (2) which processes/departments/products it touches, (3) audit/communication context. The voice + hedging rules layer on top, not replace.

### No Regression

8. All existing 16.4 acceptance criteria remain satisfied — list size 40–80, grouping into Swedish group names, no duplicates, idempotent re-runs, etc.

9. Imported law lists (`source: IMPORT` or `source: MANUAL`) are unaffected — this story only changes generation-time text.

### Test Coverage

10. Unit test in `tests/unit/agent/skills/generate-law-list.test.ts` MUST assert that the `SYSTEM_PROMPT` string contains: (a) the explicit voice rule (substring match on "Skrivton" + "Ni"), (b) the forbidden-list (substring match on "Vi", "Bolaget", "företagsnamnet"), (c) the three tiered examples (substring match on "Som aktiebolag", "bedöms Ni", "Beroende på"), (d) the hedge keyword list (substring match on "bedöms, sannolikt, kan beröra"), (e) the verification close phrasings.

### Why this is "Ni" and not "Vi" (decision rationale)

This was discussed before drafting. Short version: "Vi" reads as the company speaking about itself, which is wrong because (a) the company hasn't reviewed the text yet, and (b) it manufactures false self-statements when the AI is wrong. "Ni" is the standard B2B Swedish advisory voice — Laglig speaking *to* the customer — and pairs naturally with hedge language. If the customer later edits the text, they can flip to "Vi" themselves; the model defaults to the safer voice.

### Why hedging is structural, not cosmetic

When an inferred law (e.g., a niche miljölag the model surfaced from a single search hit) turns out not to apply, the difference between "Ni omfattas av X" (definitive) and "Beroende på hur Ni hanterar Y kan X vara tillämplig" (conditional) is the difference between Laglig having given wrong advice and Laglig having flagged something for the customer to evaluate. The latter is defensible; the former is not. Hedge keywords ARE the legal protection — they're not softness, they're calibration.
