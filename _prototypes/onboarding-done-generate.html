Why side-by-side: mirrors the laglistor-tab pattern the user just spent 4 min reading during the tutorial. The eye learns "left = explanation, right = real product surface" — and now sees the actual generated list previewed on the right with the explanation/CTA on the left.

Trust signals on the left: hero number anchors the "what we did" claim; the 3 sage-icon benefits pitch product value (skräddarsytt, ansvar/bevis, AI-bevakning) rather than just listing what the agent did; hedging line keeps the "bedöms" legal cover; "Bedöms vara relevanta… granska och justera fritt" frames it as user-reviewable, not user-binding.

Realistic preview on the right: toolbar with the actual generated count + område count, one open group (Arbetsmiljö) with 3 rows showing how the businessContext field reads ("Skräddarsydd kontext: Ni omfattas som arbetsgivare inom hotellbranschen…"), 3 collapsed groups, footer hint indicating more below. This previews the surface the primary CTA leads to.

Trade-offs vs current centered card: more horizontal real estate used (good for the 1200px modal), product-pitch tone (vs. process-pitch in current "Detta gjorde vi / Detta händer nu"), no Områden chip-row needed (the preview IS the breakdown), preview makes the modal feel substantial without synthetic content.
