4 vertical bars, fill count = priority. No color tint on the card at all. Communicates ordinality (1 < 2 < 3 < 4) instead of just category — a 4-bar fill literally looks more urgent than a 2-bar fill. Bauhaus / pro-tool aesthetic.

My read

If you want one strong opinion: → T6 critical-only. The board is calm by default and the only thing screaming at you is the thing that should. It's the most "AI-era" answer because it treats screen real estate as expensive — most cards earn no chrome.

If you want something safe and pretty: → T2 soft glow. Reads as Linear-tier polish without being aggressive. Zero learning curve — users still understand instantly.

If you want the badge to do all the heavy lifting and the card to add meaning beyond it: → T4 pips. The pip count is ordinal info you can't get from a single-color badge. Slightly busier, but each card teaches you the priority system without you having to memorise the colour palette.

Worth ruling out: T3 corner bloom looks great but is hard to do well at low-priority — too subtle to register; T5 top-edge bar is a small refinement of T1 rather than a real shift; T7 whole-card tint risks visual chaos when the column is dense.
